### Understanding the Challenge

Teaching math bilingually can introduce confusion due to linguistic differences in terminology and syntax. For instance, mathematical terms often vary across languages, leading to misunderstandings. A study by the University of Barcelona highlighted issues with false cognates—words that sound similar in two languages but have different meanings. For example, the Spanish word "dividir" resembles the English "divide," yet its contextual usage can vary, causing confusion in mathematical instructions. Furthermore, syntax differences can disrupt comprehension; in Japanese, the sentence structure often places mathematical operations differently than in English, complicating problem-solving. Educational research on cross-linguistic interference reveals that these discrepancies can hinder a child's ability to grasp mathematical concepts, impacting their cognitive development and problem-solving skills.
