### The Evolution of African Toys

Traditional African toys have long been an integral part of cultural expression and heritage, reflecting the continent's rich history and diversity. These toys often originated from natural materials readily available in local environments, such as wood, clay, and plant fibers, demonstrating resourcefulness and creativity. For example, traditional Yoruba dolls, known as "ere ibeji," were crafted to represent twins, a significant cultural phenomenon in Yoruba culture. These dolls served not only as playthings but also as spiritual objects thought to protect and honor the spirits of twins.

Over time, African toys have evolved, bridging traditional designs with contemporary influences to appeal to modern markets. This transition has been driven by globalization and increased access to various materials and manufacturing techniques.
