## Neurological Advantages of Bilingualism

Bilingualism changes the brain. It reshapes how the brain learns languages. In many African places, multilingualism is the norm. Bilingual people show strong executive function. Bilingualism can even delay diseases that hurt the brain. Brain scans show that bilingual people have more grey matter. This grey matter sits in areas that help language and control actions. These scientific findings prove that bilingualism helps the brain in many ways. This is especially true in Africa, where many languages mix.

Here are some key points:

- **Executive Function:** Bilingual brains are good at planning and decision-making.
