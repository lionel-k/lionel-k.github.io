### Making Language Part of Everyday Life

Imagine waking up each morning to the sound of your native language, a beautiful melody that connects you and your child to your roots. As the only African language family in your neighborhood, integrating your heritage language into daily routines can be both enriching and empowering. Start with simple steps, like using your native language for greetings and during meal times. This regular exposure helps strengthen your child's language skills naturally, creating a comforting familiarity with words and phrases.

To make this even more engaging, consider labeling household items in your native language. This visual reinforcement helps build vocabulary and can turn every room into a learning space. For instance, placing a label on the refrigerator or the dining table can spark curiosity and encourage conversations in your heritage tongue. Some families also find success by incorporating bilingual shopping lists, turning grocery trips into impromptu language lessons. These small changes are not just about language acquisition but also about weaving your culture into the fabric of daily life, reinforcing a sense of belonging and pride in your heritage.

### Storytime with Heritage

By immersing them in stories from home, you nurture an appreciation for their roots and offer them a sense of identity that they can carry with pride.
