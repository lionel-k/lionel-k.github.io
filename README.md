# Lingu.Africa

Preserving African heritage through language and connection.

## Our Mission

**Preserving African heritage through language by helping families in the diaspora teach their children African languages through bilingual/trilingual books, and by connecting people to their cultural roots while making learning fun and accessible for all ages.**

We are here to create beautifully designed bilingual and trilingual books (and eventually digital tools) that build bridges across generations, making it possible for children everywhere to grow up proud of where they come from. Our books are crafted to be engaging, culturally relevant, and easy to integrate into daily life so that heritage learners can maintain—and celebrate—their family languages.

## Our Audience

- **Parents in the African diaspora** who want their children to learn an African language in a fun and respectful way
- **Heritage learners of all ages** seeking to reconnect with their cultural identity
- **Language learners worldwide** curious about African languages
- **Educators and language organizers** looking for contemporary teaching materials
- **Community organizations** dedicated to language preservation

## The Problem We Are Solving

**African languages are at high risk of being lost in the diaspora.** Children growing up away from home often lack consistent exposure, resources, and engaging materials. When they can't learn (or retain) their mother tongue, the gap within families widens. This leads to:

- Cultural disconnection and loss of identity
- Difficulty communicating with extended family
- Diminished pride in one's own heritage

As a result, a rich cultural heritage is left silent—something we are determined to change.

## Our Solution

We create **bilingual and trilingual books** that make learning African languages fun, personal, and relevant.

Each book is:

- **Beautifully illustrated** to capture a child's imagination and keep them engaged
- **Bilingual or trilingual** so kids can learn side‑by‑side with English translations
- **Full of simple, relatable stories** that encourage daily use
- **Supplemented with audio support** to improve accuracy in pronunciation

We believe culture and language are woven together, and that learning your mother tongue should be a source of delight, not a chore.

## Our Focus Areas

### 1. Technical Work

- Writing, illustrating, and designing high‑quality bilingual and trilingual books
- Collaborating with native speakers and cultural experts to ensure translations are accurate and authentic
- Developing **digital tools** (online resources, interactive flashcards, audio content) that extend the books' reach and convenience
- Building a modern, easily‑maintained **web platform** (Next.js, React) for distribution and community building

### 2. Marketing

- **Sharp, well‑branded short‑form content** that raises awareness of the importance of African languages and draws parents to Lingu.Africa (e.g., @Lingu.Africa posts)
- **Distribution** through Amazon and our website, ensuring a smooth sales experience for all customers
- **Community‑focused marketing** that invites families, educators, and organizations to share stories, successes, and feedback with one another
- **Building partnerships** with schools, libraries, and cultural organizations to widen access and make African language learning commonplace

## Repository Structure

This repository contains the source code for the Lingu.Africa website (`/www`), built with Next.js 15, React, and TypeScript. The site showcases our books, provides learning resources, and hosts our blog.

### Key Directories

- `/www/app` – Next.js app router pages
- `/www/components` – React components
- `/www/content` – Blog posts and static content
- `/www/lib` – Utilities, configuration, and constants
- `/www/public` – Images, audio, and static assets

## Development

To run the website locally:

```bash
cd www
npm install
npm run dev
```

Open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000) in your browser.
